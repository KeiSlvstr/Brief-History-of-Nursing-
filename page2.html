HISTORY OF NURSING







Florence Nightingale

On May 12, it is the birth time of a great woman. We write this editorial for her honor. Florence Nightingale, the founder of modern nursing of professional nursing, was born in Florence, Italy, on 1820, in an English family; she was named of the city of her birth. Florence learned mathematics, language, philosophy and religion (all subjects that later influenced on her work) from her father.

Florence from childhood loved God and holy Book. According to her, on the seventh of February 1837, while she was walking in a garden, she heard the voice of God, which was call her to a specific mission.

Florence in 1844 decided to work at the hospital. But, her parents were opposed to this idea. In England in the middle of the nineteen century, nursing was not a decent job. In July 1850, she went to Germany and France and worked as a volunteer in hospitals. Then in 1853, she returned to London and worked as a manager in the hospital that was called Institute of the sick women. A year later, something happened that opened a new way in the Florence life.
